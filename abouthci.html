HCI Learning

Key Concepts

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) focuses on the design and use of computer technology, focusing particularly on the interfaces between people and computers. Three key principles—usability, accessibility, and user-centered design—stand out as pillars in this field. Usability is the cornerstone of HCI, focusing on the effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction of user interactions with a system. It prioritizes intuitive design, streamlined workflows, and minimizing cognitive load to enhance user productivity and satisfaction. Accessibility ensures equal access to digital systems for users with diverse abilities and disabilities. By adhering to standards like the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG).




Methodologies

Some common methodologies in HCI include:

  • User Research
  • Prototyping
  • Usability Testing
  • User-Centered Design

Explore real-world examples of successful HCI implementations, such as:

Learning Resources

Here are some recommended resources to further your understanding of HCI:

  • Books: "The Design of Everyday Things" by Don Norman, "Designing Interfaces" by Jenifer Tidwell
  • Online Courses: Coursera's HCI Specialization, Interaction Design Foundation courses
  • Articles: ACM's "Interactions" magazine, Nielsen Norman Group's articles on usability
